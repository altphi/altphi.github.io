

> Faith means belief in something concerning which doubt is still
> theoretically possible; and as the test of belief is willingness to act, one
> may say that faith is the readiness to act in a cause the prosperous issue
> of which is not certified to us in advance. It is in fact the same moral
> quality which we call courage in practical affairs; and there will be a very
> widespread tendency in men of vigorous nature to enjoy a certain amount of
> uncertainty in their philosophic creed, just as risk lends a zest to worldly
> activity.

William James, _The Sentiment of Rationality_.
