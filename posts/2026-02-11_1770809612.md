

> Nothing is more absurd than to conclude that whatever cannot be proved true,
> must therefore be regarded as false.  That which is less evident, is proved by
> that which is more evident.  But when the fact alleged is of itself of the
> highest degree of evidence we can have, it is incapable of proof.  What is
> more evident than the circular appearance of the sun?  Yet how can I prove to
> myself or to another, that the sun appears to me of a circular form?  But
> facts of this kind need no proof. EVERY FACT IS INCAPABLE OF PROOF JUST IN
> PROPORTION TO ITS CERTAINTY.

Brownson, _Synthetic Philosophy_
